What is an IP address?

An IP address (Internet Protocol) is a numeric sequence that uniquely identifies a device on a network. It can be local (inside your home or office) or global (visible on the Internet). Just like your house has a postal address, your device has an IP to send and receive data.

Main differences between public and private IPs

Internet visibility

A public IP is visible on the Internet and assigned by your provider. A private IP is internal, only visible within your local network (like your router, PC, or phone).

Public IPs are assigned by your Internet Service Provider (ISP). Private IPs are assigned by your router or local system via DHCP.

Common uses

  • Private IP: Communication between devices in your local network like printers, mobiles, or PCs.
